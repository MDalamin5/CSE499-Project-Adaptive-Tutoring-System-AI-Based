#### **Variant 2 (Clarification Needed for Addition/Subtraction)**

**Student**: Simplify \( 2x^2 + 3x + 5 \).  
**System**: The expression \( 2x^2 + 3x + 5 \) is already simplified because the terms are not like terms and cannot be combined. Do you understand this step?  
**Student**: Why can’t we add these terms?  
**System**: Good question! In algebra, you can only combine like terms. Terms are like if they have the same variable and exponent. For example, \( 2x^2 \) and \( 3x^2 \) are like terms, but \( 2x^2 \) and \( 3x \) are not because their exponents are different. Does that help?  
**Student**: Yes, it makes sense now.  
**System**: Great! The simplified expression is \( 2x^2 + 3x + 5 \). Would you like to try another problem?


*** Remove the brackets and simplify \((x + 2)^2 + (2x + 1)^2\)

#### **Variant 1 (Direct Understanding)**

**Student**: Simplify \((x + 2)^2 + (2x + 1)^2\).  
**System**: Start by expanding each squared term. First, expand \((x + 2)^2\):  
\[
(x + 2)^2 = x^2 + 4x + 4
\]  
Next, expand \((2x + 1)^2\):  
\[
(2x + 1)^2 = 4x^2 + 4x + 1
\]  
Add the expanded results:  
\[
x^2 + 4x + 4 + 4x^2 + 4x + 1
\]  
Combine like terms:  
\[
5x^2 + 8x + 5
\]  
Do you understand this step?  
**Student**: Yes, I got it.  
**System**: Great! The final simplified expression is:  
\[
5x^2 + 8x + 5
\]

\[

\]  
**Student**: Oh, I see. And for \((2x + 1)^2\)?
